Volunteer Work

BINGO Night

On Friday, May 12th, 2017 I volunteered at a BINGO night. This was my third year volunteering at this event. My job was to set up for BINGO and to clean up. One of my tasks was to organize the tables that were used to play BINGO. I completed a total of 7 hours of service time for this event.

Boy Scouts

Volunteer work is a major part of Boy Scouts. It goes along with our modo, "Do a Good Turn Daily". Service hours can range from a variety of things. One time I volunteered was during the Summer of 2016. We were putting in a Prayer garden for a Church as an Eagle Project. Some of the things we had to do were lay pavers, build a small wall and plant flowers. In all I did a total of about 12 and a half hours of service. Another example of my service to the community was attending an Honor Flight to welcome home our Countries War Veterans.

School

My program at school (Excelsior Academy) gives us an opportunity to do volunteer work. One of our most recent opportunities to gain service hours was collecting eels. This is where we collect eels from a net located where the Quassaick Creek in Newburgh NY meets the Hudson River. Once these eels are collected they are transported up streem away fron the obstacles that block their path. My school does this every Thursday during the time the eels are travling (April-May). On May 4th, I was one the students chosen to collect eels. I was there for about an hour (it was a small catch). I had a great time and can't wait to do it again next year.

Library

During the Summer of 2017, I volunteered my time at the Newburgh Free Library. At the Library, I was a program assistant in their summer program. My main job was to help the adult in charge of the program with anything that she needed. These programs lasted from 45 minutes to 1 hour at a time. One of my main duties was to help the children with any problem that they had. Most of these programs were based on the engineering process.
